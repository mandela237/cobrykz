## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** Polished and credible, but still visibly AI-assisted. Founder photography, direct copy, and the blue/ink palette add specificity. The remaining tells are the decorative grid, familiar italic-serif emphasis, repeated uppercase kickers, icon-led reassurance rows, and the recurring “claim plus checklist” cadence.

**Deterministic scan:** Two advisory `design-system-radius` findings in `app/globals.css`: a `10px` scrollbar-thumb radius and a `6px` global focus radius. The scrollbar finding is a contextual false positive; browser chrome benefits from the capsule shape. The focus radius is a real but minor token inconsistency against the documented `8px` control radius.

**Visual overlays:** No reliable user-visible overlay is available. Mutable browser injection could not be confirmed, so the detector script was not injected and no overlay is claimed.

## Overall Impression

The page now opens with authority: Mandela is visible, the headline connects web quality to real-world reputation, and the typography is materially sharper. The biggest remaining opportunity is to replace repeated assurances with evidence that could only belong to COBRYKZ. The page says “custom, direct, clear, reviewed” many times; a premium site should demonstrate those claims more often than it repeats them.

## What's Working

- **Founder accountability is tangible.** The portrait, name, first-person language, and direct-access promise separate COBRYKZ from anonymous agencies.
- **The opening proposition is immediate.** Visitors can understand the audience, problem, and desired outcome without decoding agency language.
- **Mobile is genuinely recomposed.** Readable type, focused disclosure patterns, and a thumb-accessible action rail make it more than a compressed desktop page.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Trust claims need inspectable proof

**Why it matters:** “Senior,” “custom,” “reviewed,” and “credible” are supported mostly by additional assertions. A skeptical business owner has little proof that could not appear on any competent studio site.

**Fix:** Place one concrete artifact near the first trust strip: an annotated build decision, a before/after, a named outcome, a sample deliverable, or a transparent quality review that shows Mandela's judgment.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable bolder`

### [P1] The primary conversion ends in a fragile `mailto:` handoff

**Why it matters:** A visitor without a configured mail client can complete the form and reach a dead end. That weakens the peak-end moment of the site's single most important journey.

**Fix:** Use a real form endpoint with clear sending, success, and recovery states. Keep a copyable email address as the fallback.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable harden`

### [P2] Repeated AI grammar weakens the brand

**Why it matters:** The page grid, italic serif emphasis, nearly universal kickers, rounded icon containers, and ruled reassurance rows resemble current generated landing-page conventions.

**Fix:** Keep one signature typographic gesture, remove the grid overlay where it is merely decorative, and vary section entrances according to their content instead of repeating the kicker-heading-copy formula.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable quieter`

### [P2] The narrative repeats promises instead of advancing objections

**Why it matters:** The trust strip, services, working agreement, standard, and founder sections revisit direct access, custom work, clarity, and review. The emotional journey plateaus after a strong opening.

**Fix:** Give every section one objection to resolve. Merge overlapping reassurance sections or turn one into concrete proof.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable distill`

### [P2] Contact placeholders likely miss accessible contrast

**Why it matters:** `placeholder:text-white/28` and `/32` are fragile on dark fields. Labels preserve usability, but placeholder guidance is still user-facing text.

**Fix:** Raise placeholder contrast to a verified 4.5:1 while keeping labels visually dominant.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable audit`

## Cognitive Load

The page has moderate cognitive load. Desktop places four navigation choices, two hero actions, three commitments, and four trust promises in one continuous decision field. The ten-section journey repeatedly asks visitors to distinguish overlapping assurances. Mobile controls simultaneous choice better, but tabs, horizontal swiping, accordions, and a persistent three-option rail accumulate interaction models. Six industries and five FAQ items are valid content sets, but the page needs clearer prioritization around the single conversion goal.

## Emotional Journey

The portrait and outcome-led headline create a strong, human opening. Reassurance builds quickly, then plateaus as later sections repeat promises rather than introduce proof. The contact section restores clarity by explaining the next step, but the external email-client handoff weakens the ending.

## Persona Red Flags

- **Jordan, first-time buyer:** Understands the offer quickly but may not know whether their project or budget is appropriate until deep in the FAQ.
- **Riley, stress tester:** Will expose the mailto failure mode, long encoded project notes, and loss of form context when the external handoff does not work.
- **Casey, distracted mobile visitor:** Benefits from the action rail but may miss horizontally hidden industries and lose context across multiple tabs and accordions.
- **Skeptical local owner:** May treat repeated promises as sales language until shown evidence that could not belong to another studio.

## Minor Observations

- The bordered secondary hero button also carries a wide shadow, producing the familiar “ghost-card” treatment.
- The desktop portrait scale and crop are excellent; on mobile the portrait consumes most of the first viewport and delays the proposition slightly.
- Icons are consistent but appear often enough to substitute for differentiated evidence.
- Standardizing the global focus radius from `6px` to `8px` would align it with the documented token system.

## Questions to Consider

- What proof could appear in the first 30 seconds that only Mandela could credibly show?
- If every reassurance paragraph disappeared, would the page itself still demonstrate the promised standard?
- Is opening an email client a sufficiently premium ending for the site's most important conversion?
- Which three sections would survive if every section had to resolve a distinct objection?
